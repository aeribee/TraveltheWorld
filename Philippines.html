El Nido

Description:
El Nido is a Philippine municipality on Palawan island. It’s known for white-sand beaches, coral reefs and as the gateway to the Bacuit archipelago, a group of islands with steep karst cliffs.

3 Days Suggested Itinerary in El Nido

Day 1: Arrival & Beach Exploration

Afternoon:

  • Head to Nacpan Beach, a postcard-perfect spot with soft sand and crystal-clear waters.
  • Visit the Twin Beaches (Nacpan & Calitang) for a serene vibe.

    • Evening:

  • Dine at Trattoria Altrove for Italian cuisine or The Nesting Table for local dishes with a modern twist.
  • Walk around El Nido town, explore quaint shops, and pick up souvenirs.

    • Day 2: Island Hopping Adventure – Tour A

    Morning to Afternoon: Experience the highlights of Tour A

  • Big Lagoon: Rent a kayak or join a group tour to navigate the majestic limestone formations.
  • Small Lagoon:Swim or snorkel in this peaceful turquoise haven.
  • Secret Lagoon: Discover a hidden gem tucked within the cliffs.
  • Shimizu Island: Snorkel and see colorful marine life.
  • Seven Commandos Beach: Unwind with refreshing coconut drinks.
  • Lunch: A BBQ feast prepared fresh during the tour on one of the islands.

    • Evening:

  • Catch the sunset at Las Cabanas Beach via a short tricycle ride from town.
  • Enjoy cocktails or dinner at Republica Sunset Bar .

    • Day 3: Island Hopping Bliss – Tour C

    Morning to Afternoon: Dive into the beauty of Tour C

  • Helicopter Island: Snorkel and admire its unique shape.
  • Hidden Beach: Swim in this secluded paradise.
  • Matinloc Shrine: Explore this historic island shrine with scenic views.
  • Secret Beach: Accessible through a small underwater opening.
  • Talisay Beach: Enjoy another snorkeling and swimming haven.
  • Lunch: Island buffet included in the tour.

    • Optional Alternatives:

  • Scuba Diving
  • Taraw Cliff Canopy Walk

    • Evening:

  • Return to El Nido town, relax at your hotel, or enjoy nightlife at Pukka Bar with live music.

    • Tips:
  • The dry season (November to May) is ideal for sunny weather and calm seas.
  • Bring enough cash in Philippine Pesos (₱). El Nido has limited ATMs, which sometimes run out of cash.
  • Book island hopping tours (Tours A, B, C, D) through reputable agencies.
  • Essentials: Reef-safe sunscreen, swimwear, water shoes, a quick-dry towel, a hat, sunglasses, and a dry bag for electronics.
