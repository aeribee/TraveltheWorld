Parliament Hill

Description:
Parliament Hill, colloquially known as The Hill, is an area of Crown land on the southern bank of the Ottawa River that houses the Parliament of Canada in downtown Ottawa, Ontario.

5 Days Suggested Itinerary in Ontorio

Day 1: Toronto (Downtown)

Morning:

  • Visit CN Tower for panoramic views of Toronto and Lake Ontario.
  • Explore Ripley’s Aquarium of Canada next to the CN Tower to see an impressive range of marine life.
  • Lunch: Head to St. Lawrence Market for a variety of fresh produce, gourmet food, and local specialties.

    • Afternooon:

  • Take a ferry to Toronto Islands for a relaxing walk or bike ride, or enjoy the beaches on Centre Island.
  • Alternatively, explore the Royal Ontario Museum (ROM) or the Art Gallery of Ontario (AGO) for some cultural immersion.

    • Evening:

  • Visit the Distillery District for a mix of boutique shopping, art galleries, and dining in a historic setting. Try local restaurants like Cluny Bistro.

    • Day 2: Niagara Falls

    Morning:

  • Depart early for Niagara Falls (about 1.5–2 hours by car or bus).
  • Visit the famous falls and take the Hornblower Niagara Cruise (seasonal) for a boat ride right up to the base of the falls.
  • Lunch: Enjoy lunch at one of the many restaurants near the falls or in Niagara-on-the-Lake, a charming town with a more relaxed atmosphere.

    • Afternooon:

  • Explore Niagara-on-the-Lake, known for its historic streets, boutique shops, and wineries. You can also visit the Niagara Parks Botanical Gardens.
  • Consider taking a wine tour in the Niagara wine region, famous for its ice wine.

    • Evening:

  • Head back to Toronto or, if you prefer, stay overnight in Niagara Falls and enjoy the illuminated falls at night.

    • Day 3: Algonquin Provincial Park

    Morning:

  • Travel to Algonquin Provincial Park (approximately 3 hours from Toronto).
  • Upon arrival, explore one of the park’s many scenic hiking trails, such as the Lookout Trail or the Canoe Lake loop.
  • Lunch: Pack a picnic or stop at the Algonquin Outfitters for lunch by the lake.

    • Afternooon:

  • Rent a canoe or kayak to explore the park’s pristine lakes. Alternatively, spend more time hiking and wildlife watching. You might spot moose, deer, and birds.
  • Visit the Algonquin Visitor Centre to learn more about the park’s ecology.

    • Evening:

  • Stay overnight at one of the park’s lodges or campgrounds to enjoy the peaceful surroundings and stargazing.

    • Day 4: Ottawa (Ontario’s Capital)

    Morning:

  • Depart for Ottawa (about 3.5–4 hours by car from Toronto).
  • Visit the Parliament Hill to see the iconic buildings and the Changing of the Guard ceremony (if visiting in the summer). You can also take a guided tour of the Parliament.
  • Lunch: Have lunch in the ByWard Market area, a lively neighborhood with fresh food, local products, and great restaurants.

    • Afternooon:

  • Explore Rideau Canal, a UNESCO World Heritage site. In the winter, you can ice skate on it, or in the warmer months, take a boat cruise or rent a bike along the canal.
  • Visit the National Gallery of Canada for world-class art exhibitions.

    • Evening:

  • Relax with dinner at one of Ottawa’s fine dining spots, such as The Whalesbone or Play Food & Wine.

    • Day 5: Kingston and Thousand Islands

    Morning:

  • Drive to Kingston (about 2 hours from Ottawa).
  • Visit Fort Henry, a 19th-century military fort offering panoramic views of the St. Lawrence River and historical reenactments.
  • Lunch: Enjoy lunch at one of Kingston’s waterfront restaurants, with views of the river and the Thousand Islands.

    • Afternooon:

  • Take a Thousand Islands cruise to see the stunning islands, lush landscapes, and the famous Boldt Castle on Heart Island.
  • Alternatively, visit the Kingston Penitentiary for a tour of the historic prison (book tickets in advance).

    • Evening:

  • Head back to Toronto or stay overnight in Kingston for a relaxed evening by the water. You could also dine in the Downtown Kingston area for a great selection of local food and drinks.


    • Tips:

  • Transport: Renting a car is the best option for traveling around Ontario, especially for day trips and exploring smaller towns and parks.
  • Best time to visit: Summer (June to August) offers the best weather and activities, especially for outdoor adventures. Fall (September to October) is also beautiful for experiencing fall foliage, especially in areas like Algonquin Park.
