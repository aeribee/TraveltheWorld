Machu Picchu

Description:
Machu Picchu is an Incan citadel set high in the Andes Mountains in Peru, above the Urubamba River valley. Built in the 15th century and later abandoned, it’s renowned for its sophisticated dry-stone walls that fuse huge blocks without the use of mortar, intriguing buildings that play on astronomical alignments and panoramic views. Its exact former use remains a mystery.

5 Days Suggested Itinerary in Peru

Day 1: Arrival in Lima & Flight to Cusco

Morning: Arrive in Lima. Depending on your arrival time, you can explore the city briefly.

  • Visit Miraflores and the Larcomar area for ocean views.
  • Try Peruvian cuisine at a local restaurant (e.g., ceviche or lomo saltado).

    • Afternooon:

  • Take a domestic flight to Cusco (approx. 1.5 hours).

    • Evening:

  • Check in to your hotel and rest to acclimate to the altitude (~3,400m/11,150 ft).
  • Explore Plaza de Armas and nearby streets for a light introduction to Cusco.

    • Day 2: Sacred Valley Exploration

    Morning: Start early and head to the Sacred Valley

  • Visit Pisac to see its vibrant market and stunning archaeological site.

    • Afternooon:

  • Continue to Ollantaytambo, a charming town with an impressive Inca fortress.
  • Board the train to Aguas Calientes (gateway to Machu Picchu).

    • Evening:

  • Arrive in Aguas Calientes, check in to your hotel, and prepare for the Machu Picchu visit the next day.

    • Day 3: Machu Picchu

    Morning:

  • Take an early bus up to Machu Picchu (or hike if you're feeling adventurous).
  • Enjoy a guided tour of the ancient Inca city and explore its stunning structures.

    • Afternooon:

  • Return to Aguas Calientes and take the train back to Cusco via Ollantaytambo.

    • Evening:

  • Arrive in Cusco and relax after a fulfilling day.

    • Day 4: Cusco City Highlights

    Morning: Explore Cusco’s historical sites.

  • Visit Qorikancha (Temple of the Sun) and Sacsayhuamán, an impressive Inca fortress.
  • Stroll through the San Blas neighborhood, known for its artisan shops and charming streets.

    • Afternooon:

  • Visit San Pedro Market for local food and souvenirs.
  • Explore Cusco Cathedral and Iglesia de la Compañía de Jesús on Plaza de Armas.

    • Evening:

  • Enjoy a traditional Andean dinner (try alpaca or trout) and attend a cultural show.

    • Day 5: Departure or Optional Activities

    Option 1:

  • Take a morning flight back to Lima and explore any remaining highlights, such as Barranco for art and bohemian vibes.

    • Option 2: If your flight is later, take a half-day trip from Cusco:

  • Moray and Maras Salt Mines: Stunning Inca agricultural terraces and salt pans.
  • Rainbow Mountain (requires a very early start if departing the same day).

    • Afternoon/Evening

  • Depart Peru.


    • Tips:

  • Acclimatization:Allow time to adjust to Cusco's high altitude. Stay hydrated and avoid heavy meals initially.
  • Train Tickets: Book your train and Machu Picchu tickets well in advance.
  • Packing: Bring layers for Cusco’s changing weather, comfortable shoes, and sun protection.
