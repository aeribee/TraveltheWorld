Colosseum

Description:
The Colosseum is an elliptical amphitheatre in the centre of the city of Rome, just east of the Roman Forum.

7 Days Suggested Itinerary in Italy

Day 1: Arrival in Rome

Morning: Arrive in Rome and check into your hotel. Begin with a leisurely walk through the city.

Afternoon: Visit the Colosseum, Roman Forum, and Palatine Hill to explore the heart of ancient Rome.

Evening: Stroll to the Piazza Venezia and toss a coin in the Trevi Fountain. Enjoy a classic Roman dinner in Trastevere, sampling cacio e pepe or carbonara.

Day 2: Explore Rome

Morning: Head to Vatican City to visit St. Peter’s Basilica, Sistine Chapel, and the Vatican Museums.

Afternoon: Explore the Pantheon, Piazza Navona, and Campo de' Fiori. Walk along the Via del Corso for shopping or people-watching.

Evening: Climb the Spanish Steps and enjoy a sunset view. Savor gelato and relax at a café.

Day 3: Tuscany - Florence

Morning: Take a high-speed train to Florence (1.5 hours). Check into your hotel.

Afternoon: Visit the Uffizi Gallery to see masterpieces by Botticelli and da Vinci. Explore the Duomo (Cathedral of Santa Maria del Fiore) and climb its dome for breathtaking views.

Evening: Dine on Tuscan classics like bistecca alla Fiorentina with a glass of Chianti wine.

Day 4: Tuscany - Day Trip to the Countryside

Morning: Take a day trip to Chianti or Val d'Orcia. Visit picturesque villages like San Gimignano or Montepulciano and enjoy wine-tasting tours at local vineyards.

Afternoon: Explore Pisa (for the Leaning Tower) or Siena (known for its stunning Piazza del Campo).

Evening: Return to Florence and enjoy a relaxed dinner.


Day 5: Milan

Morning: Take a train to Milan (2 hours). Check into your hotel and visit the Duomo di Milano, climbing to the rooftop for panoramic views.

Afternoon: Explore Galleria Vittorio Emanuele II, one of the world's oldest shopping malls, and visit Sforza Castle. Don’t miss The Last Supper by Leonardo da Vinci (book tickets in advance).

Evening: Enjoy aperitivo at Navigli, Milan’s trendy canal district.

Day 6: Venice

Morning: Take a high-speed train to Venice (2.5 hours).

Afternoon: Visit St. Mark’s Basilica, Doge’s Palace, and stroll across the Rialto Bridge. Wander through the charming alleys and piazzas.

Evening: Take a gondola ride at sunset and enjoy a Venetian dinner, sampling risotto al nero di seppia or bigoli in salsa. Take a high-speed train to Milan (2.5 hours).


Day 7: Lake Como

Morning: Travel to Lake Como from Milan (about 1 hour by train to Como or Varenna). Start with a visit to Como Cathedral (Duomo di Como), a beautiful Gothic-Renaissance church.

  • Take a walk along the Lakeside Promenade and through the charming streets of the town.
  • Ride the funicular to Brunate for stunning views of the lake and the Alps. Spend a bit of time taking photos and enjoying the scenery.


    • Afternoon: Enjoy lunch at a lakeside restaurant in Bellagio or Varenna. Try dishes like risotto al pesce persico (perch risotto) or other regional specialties.

  • Take a ferry back to Como and then return to Milan by train. You’ll be back by mid-afternoon to continue your journey or relax before your departure.

    • Tips:

  • Transport: Use Italy’s efficient high-speed trains for travel between cities. Purchase tickets in advance for savings.
  • Best time to visit: Spring (April-June) or fall (September-October) for pleasant weather and fewer crowds.
