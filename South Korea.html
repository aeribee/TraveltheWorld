Gyeongbokgung Palace

Description:
Gyeongbokgung, also known as Gyeongbokgung Palace, was the main royal palace of the Joseon dynasty.

5 Days Suggested Itinerary in Seoul

Day 1: Arrival and Introduction to Seoul

Arrival:

  • Land at Incheon International Airport. Take the AREX Express Train or airport limousine bus to your hotel.

    • Afternoon:

  • Check into your hotel and relax.
  • Head to Myeongdong for a light introduction to the city
  • Explore street food stalls and shop for Korean skincare and fashion.
  • Visit Myeongdong Cathedral, a historic landmark.

    • Evening:

  • Dinner at a Korean BBQ restaurant like Maple Tree House or Samgyeopjib.
  • Walk through Cheonggyecheon Stream for a peaceful evening.

    • Day 2: Palaces and Traditional Seoul

    Morning:

  • Visit Gyeongbokgung Palace: Watch the Changing of the Guard ceremony and tour the palace grounds.
  • Stop by the National Folk Museum of Korea , located within the palace.
  • Walk to Samcheong-dong, a charming neighborhood with cafes and boutiques.
  • Lunch: Try Tosokchon Samgyetang (ginseng chicken soup).

    • Afternoon:

  • Explore Bukchon Hanok Village and admire the traditional Korean houses (hanok).
  • Visit Insadong for traditional crafts, art galleries, and tea houses.

    • Evening:

  • Enjoy a performance like Nanta Show or Korean Traditional Music and Dance at Jeongdong Theater.
  • Dinner at a traditional restaurant in Insadong.

    • Day 3: Modern Seoul and Shopping

    Morning:

  • Head to Dongdaemun Design Plaza (DDP)to see its futuristic architecture.
  • Shop at nearbyDongdaemun Marketfor trendy fashion.
  • Lunch: Try Jjimdak (braised chicken) or a hearty Korean stew at a local eatery.

    • Afternoon:

  • Explore Gangnam:
  • Visit Starfield COEX Mall, featuring the iconic Starfield Library.
  • Stop by Bongeunsa Temple for a peaceful cultural experience.
  • Go to Lotte World Tower and visit the Seoul Sky Observatory for panoramic city views.

    • Evening:

  • Dinner at Kimbap Cheonguk or a local Korean chain for casual dining.
  • Stroll along Seoul Forest or Gangnam’s bustling streets

    • Day 4: Adventure and Scenic Views

    Morning:

  • Take a day trip to Nami Island
  • Lunch: Enjoy Korean fusion dishes at Nami Island’s restaurants.

    • Afternoon:

  • Return to Seoul and visit Itaewon, an international district with diverse dining options.
  • Explore the Leeum Samsung Museum of Art for contemporary and traditional Korean art.

    • Evening:

  • Dinner in Itaewon at a global or fusion cuisine restaurant.
  • Relax at a local jjimjilbang (Korean spa), such as Dragon Hill Spa.

    • Day 5: Markets, Towers, and Departure

    Morning:

  • Visit Gwangjang Market for authentic Korean street food like bindaetteok (mung bean pancakes) and gimbap.
  • Shop for souvenirs.

    • Afternoon:

  • Head to N Seoul Tower at Mount Namsan
  • Lunch: Dine at HanCook Restaurant in N Seoul Tower for a meal with a view.

    • Tips:
  • Get a T-money card for easy access to subways and buses.
  • Use the KakaoMap or Naver Map apps for navigation.
  • Respect local customs, such as bowing slightly when greeting and removing shoes when entering traditional spaces.
  • Learn basic Korean phrases like Annyeonghaseyo (Hello) and Gamsahamnida (Thank you). Most locals in tourist areas speak basic English.
