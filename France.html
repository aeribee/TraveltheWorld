Eiffel Tower

Description:
The Eiffel Tower is a wrought-iron lattice tower on the Champ de Mars in Paris, France. It is named after the engineer Gustave Eiffel, whose company designed and built the tower from 1887 to 1889.

7 Days Suggested Itinerary in France

Day 1: Arrival in Paris

Morning: Arrive in Paris. Check into your accommodation.

Afternoon: Take a leisurely walk around the Champs-Élysées, visit the Arc de Triomphe, and explore Place de la Concorde.

Evening: Have dinner in a local bistro and take a night-time cruise on the Seine River, enjoying views of the Eiffel Tower lit up.

Day 2: Explore Paris

Morning: Visit the Louvre Museum to see the Mona Lisa, Venus de Milo, and many more masterpieces.

Afternoon: Explore the Notre-Dame Cathedral and the Latin Quarter. Stroll through the Luxembourg Gardens.

Evening: Head to Montmartre to see the Sacré-Cœur Basilica and enjoy the charming neighborhood, including a café dinner at Place du Tertre.

Day 3: Versailles Day Trip

Morning: Take a short trip to Versailles to visit the Palace of Versailles and its stunning gardens.

Afternoon: Explore the palace grounds, including the Hall of Mirrors, Grand Trianon, and the Queen’s Hamlet.

Evening: Return to Paris, enjoy a traditional French dinner, and perhaps catch a show at the Moulin Rouge.

Day 4: Travel to the Loire Valley

Morning: Travel to Tours or Amboise in the Loire Valley (about 2-3 hours by train).

Afternoon:Visit Château de Chambord or Château de Chenonceau, two of the most famous castles in the area.

Evening: Stay in a local vineyard or charming bed and breakfast, and enjoy a glass of Loire wine with dinner.


Day 5: Explore More of the Loire Valley

Morning: Visit the Château de Villandry, known for its beautiful gardens.

Afternoon: Explore the charming town of Amboise and visit Clos Lucé, the home of Leonardo da Vinci.

Evening: Have dinner at a traditional restaurant in Amboise or Tours, where you can sample local dishes like rillettes or tarte Tatin.

Day 6: Head to Provence (Avignon or Aix-en-Provence)

Morning: Travel to Avignon or Aix-en-Provence (about 3-4 hours by train).

Afternoon: In Avignon, visit the Palace of the Popes and stroll across the famous Pont Saint-Bénézet. If you're in Aix-en-Provence, enjoy its beautiful squares and fountains, and visit the Saint-Sauveur Cathedral.

Evening: Enjoy dinner in a Provençal restaurant, where you can try ratatouille or bouillabaisse.


Day 7: Lavender Fields & Departure

Morning: Depending on the time of year (summer), visit the famous lavender fields of Provence near Sault or Valensole.

Afternoon: If you’re flying out of Marseille, take a short visit to the Old Port and Notre-Dame de la Garde.

Evening: Depart from Marseille Provence Airport.

Tips:

  • Transport: Consider purchasing a France Rail Pass for flexibility when traveling by train.
  • Make time to try regional specialties in each area (e.g., croissants in Paris, wine in the Loire Valley, herbs in Provence).
  • If you have more time, consider adding a trip to the French Riviera (Nice, Cannes, or Monaco) or exploring Normandy (Mont Saint-Michel, D-Day beaches).
