Corcovado Mountain

Description:
Corcovado which means "hunchback" in Portuguese, is a mountain in central Rio de Janeiro, Brazil. It is a 710-metre granite peak located in the Tijuca Forest, a national park. Corcovado hill lies just west of the city center but is wholly within the city limits and visible from great distances.

7 Days Suggested Itinerary in Brazil

Day 1: Rio de Janeiro

Morning: Arrive in Rio de Janeiro. Check into your accommodation.

Afternoon: Visit Copacabana and Ipanema beaches for a relaxing start.

Evening: Watch the sunset at Arpoador Rock. Try local dishes like feijoada or pão de queijo at a nearby eatery.

Day 2: Explore Rio's Iconic Landmarks

Morning: Take the train to Corcovado Mountain to visit the Christ the Redeemer statue

Afternoon: Visit Sugarloaf Mountain (Pão de Açúcar) for panoramic views

Evening: Stroll through Lapa and see the Selarón Steps. Enjoy live samba music in a local bar.

Day 3: Day Trip from Rio

Option 1: Visit Tijuca National Park for hiking and waterfalls

Option 2: Take a trip to Ilha Grande (~2–3 hours from Rio) for pristine beaches and laid-back vibes.

Evening: Return to Rio for a relaxed dinner.

Day 4: Flight to Iguazu Falls

Morning: Take a domestic flight to Foz do Iguaçu (~2 hours).

Afternoon: Explore the Brazilian side of Iguazu Falls. The views here offer a grand overview of the falls.

Evening: Check in to a budget-friendly hostel or hotel.


Day 5: Iguazu Falls (Argentinian Side)

Morning: Cross the border into Argentina to explore the falls up close. Walk along trails like the Devil’s Throat for breathtaking views.

Afternoon: Return to Foz do Iguaçu. Optionally, visit the Bird Park

Evening: Relax after a full day of exploration.

Day 6: Flight to Salvador

Morning: Fly to Salvador (~4-hour flight)

Afternoon: Explore the Pelourinho, Salvador’s historic center and a UNESCO site. Wander through its colorful streets and visit churches like São Francisco.

Evening: Enjoy Afro-Brazilian cuisine and music in a local restaurant. Try acarajé, a traditional street food.


Day 7: Beaches & Culture in Salvador

Morning: Visit Praia do Forte or Itapuã Beach for a relaxing morning by the sea.

Afternoon: Learn about Afro-Brazilian heritage at the Museu Afro-Brasileiro.

Evening: Depart from Salvador or enjoy one last evening of music and culture.

Tips:

  • Transport: Use public transportation or apps like Uber for local travel.
  • Activities: Many beaches and parks are free or have low entrance fees. Pre-book tickets online for discounts.
  • Accommodation: Opt for hostels, guesthouses, or Airbnb.
