Empire State Building

Description:
The Empire State Building is a 102-story Art Deco skyscraper in the Midtown South neighborhood of Manhattan in New York City. The building was designed by Shreve, Lamb & Harmon and built from 1930 to 1931. Its name is derived from "Empire State", the nickname of the state of New York.

7 Days Suggested Itinerary in New York

Day 1: New York City - Iconic Landmarks

Morning:

  • Start with a visit to Central Park . Walk around, rent a bike, or take a relaxing rowboat ride on The Lake.
  • Head to The Metropolitan Museum of Art (The Met) and explore its vast collection of art from around the world.

    • Afternooon:

  • Visit Times Square , the bustling heart of NYC. Explore the shops, see the bright lights, and maybe grab a bite at one of the nearby restaurants.
  • Walk down to Rockefeller Center and head to the Top of the Rock observation deck for incredible views of the city and Central Park.

    • Evening:

  • Watch a Broadway show in the Theater District. Some popular shows include The Lion King, Hamilton, or Wicked.
  • After the show, stroll through Broadway or grab dinner at Carmine’s or Joe’s Pizza for a classic NYC meal.

    • Day 2: New York City - Museums & Neighborhoods

    Morning:

  • Visit The Statue of Liberty and Ellis Island. Take the ferry from Battery Park for an informative and historical experience.
  • Alternatively, visit the 9/11 Memorial & Museum to pay respects and learn about the events of that tragic day.

    • Afternooon:

  • Explore the neighborhoods of Chinatown and SoHo. Walk through narrow streets, visit the local shops, and try some authentic dim sum or dumplings for lunch.
  • Visit Greenwich Village, known for its bohemian atmosphere, cafes, and quaint boutiques.

    • Evening:

  • Walk across the Brooklyn Bridge to reach Brooklyn Heights and enjoy the beautiful skyline views of Manhattan.
  • Dinner in DUMBO (Down Under the Manhattan Bridge Overpass) for trendy restaurants like The River Café with views of the bridge and skyline.

    • Day 3: Upstate New York – The Catskills

    Morning:

  • Drive or take a train to the Catskill Mountains (2-3 hours from NYC). Begin with a scenic hike to Kaaterskill Falls, one of the most iconic waterfalls in the region.
  • Alternatively, head to the Hudson River School Art Trail, which showcases the breathtaking landscapes that inspired the famous American painters.

    • Afternooon:

  • Explore the small town of Woodstock, known for its artsy vibe and historic role in the 1960s music scene. Visit local galleries, shops, and enjoy a casual lunch.
  • If you enjoy outdoor activities, consider zip-lining or kayaking on the Hudson River or take a scenic drive through the mountains.

    • Evening:

  • Have dinner at a local inn or farm-to-table restaurant. Enjoy a relaxed evening in the Catskills, perhaps staying in a cozy cabin or historic B&B.

    • Day 4: Hudson Valley – Historic Mansions & Wineries

    Morning:

  • Visit Vanderbilt Mansion or FDR’s Home in Hyde Park for a touch of history.
  • Alternatively, explore Storm King Art Center, an outdoor sculpture park.

    • Afternooon:

  • Lunch at a Hudson Valley restaurant, then enjoy a wine tasting at a local winery like Brotherhood Winery.
  • Explore the charming town of Beacon or Cold Spring.

    • Evening:

  • Stay in the Hudson Valley and enjoy a peaceful dinner in Rhinebeck or another nearby town.

    • Day 5: The Adirondacks & Lake Placid

    Morning:

  • Drive up to the Adirondack Mountains (approximately 3-4 hours from the Catskills). Visit Lake Placid, a charming village that was the site of two Winter Olympics.
  • Take the High Falls Gorge trail for beautiful views of the falls and a great outdoor experience.

    • Afternooon:

  • Explore Lake Placid’s Mirror Lake. You can walk around the lake, rent a kayak, or take a boat tour.
  • For nature lovers, hike one of the Adirondack High Peaks for stunning views of the surrounding mountains. If you're interested in the Olympics, you can also visit the Lake Placid Olympic Museum

    • Evening:

  • Enjoy dinner at a local restaurant like The Mirror Lake Inn or The Cottage Café.
  • Consider staying overnight in Lake Placid, where you can enjoy a cozy evening by the lake or by a fire at your hotel.

    • Day 6: Niagara Falls

    Morning:

  • Take a flight or drive to Niagara Falls (approximately 4-5 hours from Lake Placid).
  • Begin your day by visiting the falls and getting up close with a Hornblower Niagara Cruise (seasonal) to experience the power of the falls.

    • Afternooon:

  • Explore Niagara Parks Botanical Gardens or take a walk-through Queen Victoria Park.
  • Cross over to the Canadian side for some of the best views of the falls (make sure you have your passport). You can also visit Journey Behind the Falls for an exciting underground experience.

    • Evening:

  • Dine with a view of the illuminated falls. Restaurants like Skylon Tower Revolving Dining Room provide a memorable experience.

    • Day 7: Return to New York City – Empire State Building

    Morning:

  • Drive or fly back to New York City. Start your day by visiting the Empire State Building. Take the elevator to the 86th-floor observation deck for iconic views of Manhattan.
  • Begin your day by visiting the falls and getting up close with a Hornblower Niagara Cruise (seasonal) to experience the power of the falls.

    • Afternooon:

  • Explore Bryant Park or enjoy a walk along the High Line, an elevated park offering unique views of the city.

    • Evening:

  • Wrap up your trip with dinner at a classic New York restaurant like Katz’s Delicatessen (famous for its pastrami sandwiches) or an upscale spot like Le Bernardin.


    • Tips:

  • Transport: Renting a car is recommended for getting around Upstate New York. For NYC, you can rely on public transportation (subways, buses) and walking.
  • Best time to visit: Late spring to fall (May to October) offers pleasant weather for outdoor activities. Winter (December to February) is great for snow sports, especially in the Adirondacks.
